# Mid-Semester Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title
Mental Health Literature Review AI Assistant

## Slide 2: Project Motivation
- Literature reviews are time-consuming.
- Mental health research is broad and constantly growing.
- AI may help researchers organize and summarize papers more efficiently.

## Slide 3: Research Question
How can agent-based AI tools help organize and summarize mental health research literature more efficiently?

## Slide 4: Project Scope
- Focus on mental health research literature.
- Topics include depression, anxiety, sleep, stress, and student well-being.
- Current stage focuses on a baseline pipeline.

## Slide 5: Data Source
- Initial dataset of mental health literature records.
- Fields include title, year, journal, topic, and abstract.
- Future work will expand the dataset using public databases such as PubMed.

## Slide 6: Methodology
- Load dataset.
- Clean abstract text.
- Generate baseline summaries.
- Extract keywords.
- Evaluate summary metrics.
- Export results.

## Slide 7: Current Progress
- Research question finalized.
- GitHub repository created.
- Initial dataset added.
- Baseline pipeline implemented.
- Documentation and project structure created.
- Evaluation metrics started.

## Slide 8: Preliminary Observations
- The baseline pipeline can organize article records and create short summaries.
- Rule-based summaries are useful as a starting point but may miss context.
- More advanced LLM methods are needed for better synthesis.

## Slide 9: Challenges
- Learning agent-based AI terminology and tools.
- Keeping project scope realistic.
- Evaluating summary quality is not always straightforward.
- Mental health research requires careful interpretation.

## Slide 10: Next Steps
- Expand the dataset.
- Integrate LLM-generated summaries.
- Compare baseline and LLM results.
- Add visualizations.
- Prepare final analysis and report.
